## How to Choose Free Courses

### Quality Indicators

✅ **Check the instructor:**
- Professors from top universities
- Industry experts with credentials
- Positive student reviews

✅ **Review the syllabus:**
- Comprehensive curriculum
- Practical projects
- Updated content

✅ **Read student reviews:**
- 4+ star rating
- Recent reviews (content is current)
- Mentions of career impact

### Free vs. Paid Certificates

| Feature | Free Audit | Paid Certificate |
|---------|-----------|------------------|
| Video Lectures | ✅ Yes | ✅ Yes |
| Reading Materials | ✅ Yes | ✅ Yes |
| Quizzes | ✅ Usually | ✅ Yes |
| Graded Assignments | ❌ No | ✅ Yes |
| Certificate | ❌ No | ✅ Yes |
| Financial Aid | N/A | ✅ Available |

**Bottom Line:** Audit for free to learn, pay only if you need the certificate for your resume.

## Common Questions

### Are free courses really the same quality?

**Yes!** The video lectures, materials, and content are identical to paid versions. You're learning from the same professors with the same curriculum.

### Will employers respect free certificates?

**It depends:**
- Skills matter more than certificates
- Project portfolio proves competence
- From top universities = more credible
- Verified certificates carry more weight

**Pro tip:** Combine free learning with practical projects to demonstrate mastery.

### Can I get college credit?

**Sometimes:**
- Some MOOCs offer credit pathways
- ACE recommendations on select courses
- Check with your institution
- May need to pay for proctored exams

### How long do I have access?

**Varies by platform:**
- **Coursera:** Audit access often limited after course ends
- **edX:** Varies by course
- **MIT OpenCourseWare:** Forever
- **Khan Academy:** Forever

**Solution:** Download materials during course period!
