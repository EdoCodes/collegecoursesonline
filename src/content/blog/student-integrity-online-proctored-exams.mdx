<div class="section-label">Overview</div>

Online proctored exams have become a permanent part of higher education, and student integrity is now central to how colleges evaluate learning. As remote programs have grown, institutions have relied more heavily on proctoring tools to verify identity, deter cheating, and protect the credibility of grades and transfer credit.{' '}
<a class="link-external" href="https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC8407138/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">NIH/PMC open-access review on online exam supervision</a>.

At the same time, the expansion of online testing has created a market for cheating services that advertise help taking exams on behalf of students. That trend makes academic integrity even more important, not less.{' '}
<a class="link-external" href="https://www.naxlex.com/pay-someone-to-take-my-proctored-exam" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Industry reporting on paid exam-taking offers (Naxlex)</a>.

<div class="section-label">Foundations</div>

## What student integrity means

Student integrity means completing academic work honestly, independently, and according to the rules of the course. In the context of online proctored exams, that includes not using unauthorized materials, not receiving outside help, and not allowing another person to take the exam.{' '}
<a class="link-external" href="https://files.eric.ed.gov/fulltext/EJ1338047.pdf" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">ERIC: systematic review on cheating in online exams (PDF)</a>.

Integrity matters because a proctored exam is supposed to measure what a student knows. If the score comes from cheating, impersonation, or hidden assistance, then the result no longer reflects real learning.{' '}
<a class="link-external" href="https://www.insidehighered.com/news/2022/04/28/study-online-exam-cheating" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Inside Higher Ed: study on online exam cheating trends</a>.

## Why online proctored exams keep growing

Online proctored exams are no longer a temporary workaround. Research and institutional guidance show that online assessment has become embedded in higher education, especially in fully online and hybrid programs.{' '}
<a class="link-external" href="https://socialsci.libretexts.org/Bookshelves/Education_and_Professional_Development/Learning_in_the_Digital_Age_(Asino_et_al.)/01:_Chapters/1.04:_Virtual_Proctoring_and_Academic_Integrity" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">LibreTexts: virtual proctoring and academic integrity</a>.

Colleges use proctoring because they need a way to preserve exam credibility when students are not physically present in a classroom or testing center. In that environment, identity checks, browser restrictions, and video monitoring are often used to reduce the risk of dishonesty.{' '}
<a class="link-external" href="https://elearningindustry.com/ethical-online-exam-proctoring-guiding-principles-and-best-practices" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">eLearning Industry: ethical online exam proctoring principles</a>.

## Why integrity is essential

Integrity is what gives an exam its value. A final exam is not just a hoop to jump through; it is a test of competence, preparation, and mastery.{' '}
<a class="link-external" href="https://open.library.okstate.edu/learninginthedigitalage/chapter/virtual-proctoring-and-academic-integrity/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Oklahoma State open textbook: virtual proctoring and academic integrity</a>.

When integrity is compromised, several harms follow:

- Honest students are disadvantaged.
- Grades become less meaningful.
- Transfer credit and certificates lose credibility.
- Employers and universities may trust the credential less.{' '}
  <a class="link-external" href="https://courses.up.eku.edu/IDCWS/articles/Examining%20the%20Effect%20of%20Proctoring%20on%20Online%20Test%20Scores.pdf" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">EKU: working paper on proctoring and online test scores (PDF)</a>.

That is why colleges take integrity violations seriously and often treat them as a direct threat to academic standards.{' '}
<a class="link-external" href="https://study.com/academy/popular/studycoms-academic-integrity-policy.html" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Study.com academic integrity policy</a>.

<div class="section-label">Research</div>

## What the research says

Research suggests that cheating in online exams is real and persistent. One large proctoring study reported a confirmed breach rate of 6.6 percent across millions of exams, with higher education exams showing a 7.2 percent breach rate. A systematic review of online cheating research also found repeated evidence of behaviors such as unauthorized materials, third-party help, and exam impersonation.{' '}
<a class="link-external" href="https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC8898996/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">NIH/PMC: systematic review on cheating in online exams</a>.

Other studies suggest that proctoring can reduce cheating or at least discourage it, even if it cannot eliminate every dishonest attempt. In short, online proctoring is not perfect, but it remains one of the main tools institutions use to defend academic integrity.{' '}
<a class="link-external" href="https://www.smarterservices.com/blog/exam-cheating-and-the-need-for-virtual-proctoring" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">SmarterServices: exam cheating and virtual proctoring</a>.

## The cheating economy around proctored exams

As online exams have become more common, a parallel market has grown around cheating services. Some websites openly advertise “take my proctored exam” help, exam impersonation, or other ways to bypass academic controls.{' '}
<a class="link-external" href="https://eazyresearch.com/take-my-exam-for-me" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Example third-party site advertising exam-taking help (EazyResearch)</a>.

These services are not tutoring or legitimate academic support. They are designed to help students circumvent exam rules, and they directly undermine the purpose of proctored assessment. Their existence is one reason colleges continue tightening monitoring, authentication, and policy enforcement.{' '}
<a class="link-external" href="https://study.com/academy/popular/studycoms-academic-integrity-policy.html" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Study.com academic integrity policy</a>.

## The ethics problem

Online proctoring is meant to protect fairness, but it also raises real ethical concerns. Scholars have warned about privacy, surveillance, bias, accessibility, and due process, especially when institutions rely on automated systems or opaque review processes.{' '}
<a class="link-external" href="https://files.eric.ed.gov/fulltext/EJ1415725.pdf" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">ERIC: ethics of online exam supervision (PDF)</a>.

Students may feel watched or unfairly flagged even when they have done nothing wrong. That is why ethical proctoring requires more than software. Institutions should explain the rules clearly, protect student data, provide accommodations, and offer a meaningful appeal process when misconduct is alleged.{' '}
<a class="link-external" href="https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC8407138/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">NIH/PMC: ethics of online exam supervision</a>.

## Why institutions enforce strict rules

Colleges do not use proctoring only to punish dishonesty. They use it to protect the value of the entire academic system.{' '}
<a class="link-external" href="https://open.library.okstate.edu/learninginthedigitalage/chapter/virtual-proctoring-and-academic-integrity/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Oklahoma State open textbook: virtual proctoring and academic integrity</a>.

That matters especially for transfer credit, professional programs, and coursework that appears on a transcript. Once an institution believes an integrity violation occurred, it may decide that the credit is invalid or ineligible for transfer under its own policy. Study.com’s own policy, for example, states that repeated or egregious violations may lead to invalidation of an assessment attempt, revocation of credit, or removal from the platform.{' '}
<a class="link-external" href="https://support.study.com/support/solutions/articles/1000028396-are-study-com-courses-and-credit-by-exam-courses-transferable-to-my-college-" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Study.com Help Center: transferability and credit-by-exam</a>.

## What students should understand

Students taking online proctored exams should assume the rules are strict and that the exam environment may be monitored closely. The safest approach is to prepare thoroughly, keep the workspace clean, avoid prohibited materials, and follow all exam instructions exactly.{' '}
<a class="link-external" href="https://www.talview.com/guides/ethical-proctoring" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Talview: ethical proctoring guide for students</a>.

It is also important to recognize that “help” from another person, a website, or a hidden device can be treated as an integrity violation even if the student believes the exam was easy or harmless. In proctored testing, compliance with the rules matters as much as intent.{' '}
<a class="link-external" href="https://files.eric.ed.gov/fulltext/EJ1338047.pdf" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">ERIC: dealing with cheating in online exams (PDF)</a>.

## A practical example

Imagine two students take the same proctored final. One studies, follows the rules, and earns an honest score. The other uses unauthorized notes or hires someone else to take the exam. Both may receive a passing grade temporarily, but only one score truly reflects the course outcome. That is why integrity is the backbone of online assessment.{' '}
<a class="link-external" href="https://www.insidehighered.com/news/2022/04/28/study-online-exam-cheating" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Inside Higher Ed on online exam cheating</a>.

## Best practices for schools

Schools that want to maintain both integrity and trust should focus on several principles:

- Make exam rules easy to understand.
- Use proctoring in proportion to the stakes of the exam.
- Provide accessible accommodations.
- Explain what evidence is needed for an integrity finding.
- Offer a fair appeal process.
- Protect student privacy and data.{' '}
  <a class="link-external" href="https://www.talview.com/guides/ethical-proctoring" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Talview: ethical proctoring</a>.

This approach does not eliminate cheating, but it improves legitimacy. Students are more likely to accept proctoring when the process is transparent and consistent.{' '}
<a class="link-external" href="https://elearningindustry.com/ethical-online-exam-proctoring-guiding-principles-and-best-practices" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">eLearning Industry: guiding principles for proctoring</a>.

<div class="section-label">Conclusion</div>

## Final thoughts

Online proctored exams are likely to remain a major part of higher education, and with that comes a stronger demand for student integrity. The more testing moves online, the more important it becomes for schools to balance academic honesty with fairness, transparency, and privacy.{' '}
<a class="link-external" href="https://elearningindustry.com/ethical-online-exam-proctoring-guiding-principles-and-best-practices" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">eLearning Industry: ethical proctoring principles</a>.

For students, the lesson is simple: treat a proctored online exam as seriously as an in-person exam. For institutions, the challenge is making sure the rules are clear, the enforcement is consistent, and the process remains trustworthy.{' '}
<a class="link-external" href="https://open.library.okstate.edu/learninginthedigitalage/chapter/virtual-proctoring-and-academic-integrity/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Oklahoma State: virtual proctoring and academic integrity</a>.
