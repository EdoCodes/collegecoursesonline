## Why El Camino College stands out

El Camino College in Torrance has served the South Bay since **1947** and remains one of the region’s most important public colleges for **affordable education**, **transfer preparation**, and **career-focused health programs**. Today, one of its most notable developments is the **Bachelor of Science in Respiratory Care** offered in an **online-accelerated format**—designed for graduates who already hold an associate degree in respiratory care.

Students exploring **medical imaging** should also know that ECC offers a separate **Radiologic Technology** pathway at the **associate** level: a **hybrid-style** program with **some remote lectures and exams** plus **required in-person labs and clinical training**—not a bachelor’s in radiology earned entirely online at ECC.

For national context on picking accredited online programs, see our{' '}
<a href="/resources/blog/best-online-colleges-2026">best online colleges for 2026</a> overview after you review ECC’s official requirements.

## El Camino College history

El Camino College was officially established on **July 1, 1947**, when South Bay communities invested in a local public college for a fast-growing postwar population. Early classes used **temporary wartime barracks** before permanent facilities took shape on campus; the institution’s physical growth tracked decades of regional demand for workforce and transfer education.{' '}
<a class="link-external" href="https://www.elcamino.edu/about/history.php" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">ECC History</a>

The name **El Camino** references **El Camino Real**, the historic California mission corridor; campus imagery often reflects that heritage. Bond-supported modernization later helped ECC expand facilities and student services while preserving its core mission—access, equity, and student success.{' '}
<a class="link-external" href="https://www.elcamino.edu/leadership/bond/faq.php" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">ECC Bond FAQ</a>

## ECC today

Today, El Camino balances **transfer pathways**, **workforce programs**, and **flexible learning**. Its **online education** infrastructure supports students who need alternatives to a fully traditional schedule—especially working adults and career-advancing learners in allied health.{' '}
<a class="link-external" href="https://www.elcamino.edu/academics/online-education/index.php" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">ECC Online Education</a>

According to ECC’s general college overview, the campus spans about **126 acres** near Torrance and offers roughly **200 degree and certificate programs**, with enrollment on the order of **tens of thousands of students per semester**—making ECC a regional anchor rather than a small neighborhood college.{' '}
<a class="link-external" href="https://www.elcamino.edu/about/index.php" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">About El Camino College</a>

## Online B.S. in Respiratory Care

El Camino College offers a **Bachelor of Science in Respiratory Care** intended for students who **already completed** (or are completing) prerequisite respiratory-care education at the associate level. ECC describes the program as an **accelerated** bachelor’s delivered in an **online format** across **three semesters**, using **eight-week terms**, with a total timeline of about **18 months** for the published plan. The program is also highlighted as **ECC’s first bachelor’s degree**, launched to expand opportunity beyond the traditional community-college credential ladder.{' '}
<a class="link-external" href="https://www.elcamino.edu/academics/divisions/health-sciences-and-athletics/respiratory-care/bachelors-program.aspx" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Bachelor’s Degree in Respiratory Care (ECC)</a>{' '}
<a class="link-external" href="https://www.elcamino.edu/news/el-camino-offers-new-bachelors-degree-in-respiratory-care.php" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">ECC news: New bachelor’s in Respiratory Care</a>

Academically, ECC positions the bachelor’s as preparation for **leadership**, **education**, **policy**, and **advanced clinical practice** responsibilities—often building on the college’s long-running respiratory care associate pathway. Always confirm the **current unit total**, **course list**, and **term calendar** on ECC’s official bachelor’s program page before you budget time or money.

## Who should consider this program

This bachelor’s is a **completion-style** path—not for students seeking first-time entry into respiratory care without prior accredited training. ECC’s **admission information** outlines expectations such as completion of a **COARC-accredited** respiratory care program and an **associate degree in respiratory care** from an accredited community college, along with progress on **CSU-GE** or **IGETC** transferable general education patterns (including minimum completed units before admission).{' '}
<a class="link-external" href="https://www.elcamino.edu/academics/divisions/health-sciences-and-athletics/respiratory-care/admission-information.php" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Respiratory Care — Admission Information</a>

If that matches your background—often **working respiratory therapists** or **associate-degree graduates** aiming upward—ECC’s online-format bachelor’s can be a practical South Bay option. If you are not yet respiratory-care credentialed, start with ECC’s **associate-level respiratory care** information and licensing pathways rather than the bachelor’s page.

## Hybrid A.S. in Radiologic Technology

Students interested in **medical imaging** should track a **different ECC program** than the respiratory-care bachelor’s. ECC’s **Radiologic Technology** pathway leads to **associate-level** outcomes (and related certificate options), includes about **26 months** of structured progression, and combines **didactic learning**, **labs**, and **clinical assignments**—with ECC describing **some lectures and exams** presented **remotely** while **clinical education remains in person**.{' '}
<a class="link-external" href="https://www.elcamino.edu/academics/divisions/health-care/radiologic-technology/index.php" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Radiologic Technology (ECC)</a>{' '}
<a class="link-external" href="https://www.elcamino.edu/academics/divisions/health-care/radiologic-technology/degree-certificate.php" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Radiologic Technology — Degree &amp; Certificate</a>

**Key distinction for readers and search engines:** ECC’s **online-format bachelor’s** featured in this guide is **Respiratory Care**. ECC’s **radiologic technology** route is best described as **hybrid clinical training** toward **entry-to-practice imaging** eligibility (including ARRT-oriented preparation as described by ECC), **not** a fully online bachelor’s in radiology at El Camino College.

## Cost and format

ECC publishes bachelor’s respiratory-care planning details—including **upper-division per-unit fees**—on its official pages. Third-party summaries have cited approximate total tuition bands for the full bachelor’s unit load, but **tuition and fees change**; use ECC’s current bachelor’s and fiscal pages for budgeting and financial aid questions.{' '}
<a class="link-external" href="https://www.elcamino.edu/academics/divisions/health-sciences-and-athletics/respiratory-care/bachelors-program.aspx" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Bachelor’s program overview</a>

**Application timing** also shifts by cohort year. ECC’s admission materials typically list filing periods for upcoming fall starts—**verify the open/close dates** on the official admission page rather than relying on blog copies of older calendars.

## Why health-care students choose ECC

ECC appeals to health-care students because it pairs **public college affordability** with programs tied to **licensure and workforce outcomes**. The **Respiratory Care bachelor’s** targets upward mobility for credentialed professionals, while **Radiologic Technology** supports students entering imaging through a **hybrid clinical** model. For South Bay residents, that can mean **lower total cost** and **less disruption** than relocating for a private program—provided you meet ECC’s selective admission rules for the bachelor’s completion track.
