<div class="section-label">Overview</div>

University of the People (UoPeople) is one of the best-known tuition-free online universities in the world, offering accredited degree programs with only minimal fees rather than traditional tuition. It is designed for students who need an affordable, flexible path to a recognized degree, including learners balancing work, family, or international relocation.{' '}
<a class="link-external" href="https://www.uopeople.edu/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">University of the People — official homepage</a>

## What University of the People is

UoPeople is a nonprofit, 100% online university that describes itself as tuition-free and accredited. The university emphasizes access, affordability, and global reach, with students from more than 200 countries and territories according to third-party reporting. Its model is built around small classes, online delivery, and low administrative or assessment fees instead of standard per-credit tuition.{' '}
<a class="link-external" href="https://www.uopeople.edu/tuition-free/what-is-tuition-free/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">UoPeople: What does tuition-free mean?</a>

## Why it stands out

The biggest reason UoPeople gets attention is simple: it removes tuition as the main barrier to college. That makes it especially appealing for students who want an accredited American-style degree but cannot afford traditional university pricing. The university also positions itself as a flexible option for adult learners, international students, and people seeking a fully online academic pathway.{' '}
<a class="link-external" href="https://www.uopeople.edu/programs/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">UoPeople degree programs overview</a>

## Accreditation and recognition

University of the People (UoPeople) is regionally accredited by the{' '}
<a class="link-external" href="https://www.wscuc.org/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">WASC Senior College and University Commission (WSCUC)</a>, one of the most respected accrediting bodies in the United States. This accreditation reflects the university’s commitment to high academic standards while ensuring that degrees are widely recognized and credits are transferable. UoPeople previously held accreditation from the{' '}
<a class="link-external" href="https://www.deac.org/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">DEAC</a>{' '}
before transitioning to WSCUC as its primary accreditor.

UoPeople also describes its programs as “American-accredited” and fully online. Accreditation is what gives a degree academic legitimacy and makes transfer or employment review possible, even if individual institutions still decide whether they will accept the credits. In practice, accreditation does not guarantee universal transferability, but it does improve the chance that the degree will be evaluated seriously.{' '}
<a class="link-external" href="https://www.uopeople.edu/become-student/admissions/transferring-credits/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">UoPeople: transferring credits to UoPeople</a>

## Tuition-free model explained

When UoPeople says “tuition-free,” it means students do not pay tuition for courses, course materials, or annual enrollment. Instead, students pay modest fees as they progress through the program, including application and assessment-related fees. This structure allows the university to keep access broad while still funding operations. Third-party guides also list UoPeople among tuition-free or low-cost accredited online options—always verify current fees on UoPeople’s own site.{' '}

## How the academic structure works

UoPeople’s learning model is fully online, with students completing coursework remotely and advancing through structured terms. The university highlights academic support, including program advisors and small classes, to help students stay on track. Because the model is online and tuition-free, it is particularly appealing to students who need flexibility and low upfront cost. Independent higher-education resources also discuss tuition-free online colleges in this context.{' '}

## Costs beyond tuition

Even though tuition is free, UoPeople is not completely costless. Students typically still face application fees and assessment fees, and some third-party sources note additional exam-related expenses. UoPeople’s own materials stress that these fees are “minimal,” but prospective students should still review the full cost structure carefully before enrolling. For a plain-language overview from outside the university, see independent explainers (not a substitute for UoPeople’s official fee schedule).{' '}

## Does UoPeople accept transfer credits?

Yes, UoPeople says it may accept transfer credits from accredited U.S. and non-U.S. institutions. Its transfer policy states that each course is reviewed on a course-by-course basis, and generally must meet criteria such as a grade equivalent to C or above, equivalent content, and completion at an accredited institution. Approved credits are still subject to UoPeople’s final academic discretion.{' '}
<a class="link-external" href="https://www.uopeople.edu/wp-content/uploads/2022/11/Transfer-Credit-Policy.pdf" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">UoPeople Transfer Credit Policy (PDF)</a>

## How transfer credit evaluation works

According to UoPeople’s transfer materials, students submit transfer requests through the admissions or student portal, and the transfer credit team reviews the request. UoPeople states it usually reviews requests within 48 hours, then notifies students of the decision by portal and email. Approved courses may incur a fee per course—confirm the current amount on UoPeople’s official transfer page, as fees can change.{' '}
<a class="link-external" href="https://www.uopeople.edu/become-student/admissions/transferring-credits/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">UoPeople: transferring credits — official page</a>

## Will other schools accept UoPeople credits?

That is a separate question, and the answer is institution-specific. UoPeople states that the transferability of its credits and degrees is always at the discretion of the receiving institution. That means another college may accept all, some, or none of the credits, depending on its own policies and the destination program.{' '}
<a class="link-external" href="https://catalog.uopeople.edu/ug_term1_item/undergraduate-admissions/notice-concerning-transferability-of-credits-and-credentials-earned-at-our-institution" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">UoPeople catalog: notice on transferability of credits and credentials</a>

## Who UoPeople is best for

UoPeople tends to fit students who need affordability first, flexibility second, and brand prestige third. It may be a strong match for adult learners, career changers, working students, and international students who want a recognized U.S.-style online degree without major debt. It is also appealing to students who already have credits from another institution and want to reduce the remaining cost to finish a degree. Use independent explainers only as context alongside UoPeople’s own admissions materials.{' '}
<a class="link-external" href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zySkvtpH-5Q" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Independent overview (YouTube)</a>

## Potential limitations

The tradeoff for affordability is that students need to pay close attention to fees, transferability, and the acceptance of the degree elsewhere. Because the degree is online and UoPeople is newer than many century-old universities, some employers or schools may evaluate it differently from more familiar institutions. Prospective students should also confirm whether the exact program they want is currently offered and whether it matches their academic or career goals.{' '}
