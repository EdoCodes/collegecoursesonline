<div class="section-label">Overview</div>

## Why nursing prerequisites matter

Nursing programs are among the most competitive healthcare pathways in the country. Nursing school enrollment for entry-level BSN programs declined 4.4% in 2024, even as the U.S. projects a shortage of 200,000 to 450,000 nurses if the workforce doesn't grow significantly. Fewer spots. More applicants. Your prerequisite record matters more than ever.

Admissions committees use prerequisite grades to measure readiness — specifically whether you can handle the pace of anatomy, pharmacology, pathophysiology, and evidence-based practice all at once. Strong grades in science prerequisites like Anatomy and Physiology, Microbiology, and Chemistry often carry as much weight as your overall GPA.

But prerequisites are not just a hoop. They build the actual knowledge base you will use every day as a nurse. Cellular chemistry explains drug interactions. Physiology tells you why blood pressure drops in septic shock. Microbiology shapes how you think about infection control. The preparation is real.

## Which nursing prerequisites are the hardest?

This is one of the most common questions on r/prenursing, Allnurses, and Reddit nursing communities — and the answers are pretty consistent.

### Microbiology

Students and nursing educators frequently name Microbiology as the toughest prerequisite. The lecture content is dense — bacterial structures, immune responses, disease mechanisms — but it's the lab that surprises most students. Labs require identifying bacteria using a series of biochemical tests and often drawing detailed microscope images. Memorization alone won't carry you through. You need to understand mechanisms and apply them.

### Anatomy & Physiology I and II

A&P is the other consistent contender. The volume of material is enormous — bones, muscles, nerves, organ systems — and many students encounter it during their first semester of college before they've developed strong study habits. Students who succeed tend to use active recall strategies, redraw diagrams from memory, and break the material into small daily chunks rather than cramming before exams.

### General Chemistry

Chemistry is challenging for students who struggle with math-based reasoning. Understanding pH levels, molecular bonding, acids and bases, and biochemical reactions requires both conceptual understanding and problem-solving practice. Most nursing programs only require General Chemistry, not Organic Chemistry.

- **ADN programs:** Minimum GPA 2.0–2.8. Competitive applicants have 3.0 or higher. Many use point-based admission systems.
- **BSN programs:** Minimum GPA 3.0. Competitive applicants have 3.5 or higher. Some programs require 3.8+ for early admission.
- **ABSN programs:** Minimum GPA 3.0–3.2. Very competitive — science GPA is scrutinized heavily. Prior bachelor's degree required.

An important note that often gets buried: professional nursing schools do not honor grade forgiveness or grade replacement policies. All grades — including repeated courses — count and are averaged together. If you retake Microbiology to improve a C, both grades factor into your science GPA.

<div class="pro-tip">
  <strong>Important:</strong> Science prerequisite grades carry extra weight in nursing admissions. A 3.8 overall GPA with a C in Anatomy and Physiology will often be less competitive than a 3.3 overall with an A in every science course.
</div>

<div class="section-label">Online Options</div>

## Online course options and caveats

Online prerequisites are a real option for students managing work schedules, family commitments, or geographic limitations — but they come with important caveats.

### What online courses are typically accepted

General education subjects are where online providers like Sophia Learning and StraighterLine tend to have the best acceptance rates: English Composition, General Psychology, Sociology, Statistics, and College Algebra.

### Where online courses often fall short

Science courses with required lab components are the critical exception. Anatomy and Physiology, Microbiology, and Chemistry labs are often required to be completed in-person. An online lecture course in Microbiology without an accepted lab component will not satisfy the prerequisite at most nursing programs.

### A note on StraighterLine and Anatomy & Physiology

StraighterLine's A&P is unlikely to be accepted at most prelicensure nursing programs because of lab requirements. Students should assume it will not transfer to a traditional BSN or ADN program unless they have written confirmation from that specific program.

<div class="pro-tip">
  <strong>Before you enroll anywhere online:</strong> Email the admissions office at every nursing program you're targeting and ask: "Will [specific course name and provider] satisfy your prerequisite for [subject]?" Get the answer in writing.
</div>

<div class="section-label">Online Programs</div>

## Accredited online and hybrid nursing programs

If flexibility is a priority — whether because of work, family, or location — there are many accredited online and hybrid nursing programs offering ASN, ADN, BSN, and RN-to-BSN pathways. The important thing to understand before applying: most programs labeled "online" are actually online/hybrid. Nursing education always requires hands-on clinical experience, lab work, or practicum hours that must be completed in person. The coursework is online; the patient care is not.

With that in mind, here are accredited programs worth researching, organized by degree type.

### Online and hybrid ASN / ADN programs

These programs prepare students to sit for the NCLEX-RN exam and typically take two years to complete after prerequisites are done.

- **Herzing University** — accredited online ASN program with RN and BSN pathways available.
- **Joyce University** — accredited online ASN program.
- **University of Jamestown** — online ASN program.
- **FVI School of Nursing and Technology** — hybrid ASN program.
- **St. Joseph School of Nursing** — online ASN 2-year track.

### Online and hybrid BSN programs

BSN programs are preferred by many hospital systems and Magnet-designated facilities. These programs typically require 60+ prerequisite credits before the nursing curriculum begins.

- **University of Texas at Arlington** — online BSN and RN-to-BSN pathways, one of the largest online nursing programs in the country.
- **Chamberlain University** — online BSN, CCNE-accredited.
- **Purdue Global** — online nursing degree programs including baccalaureate pathways.
- **Western Governors University (WGU)** — online BSN and RN-to-BSN, competency-based format.
- **Baylor University** — online accelerated BSN program.
- **West Coast University** — online nursing programs, California-focused.
- **Walden University** — online nursing programs including BSN pathways.

### Online RN-to-BSN programs

RN-to-BSN programs are designed for working nurses who already hold an RN license and an ADN or diploma. Most can be completed in 12 to 18 months entirely online, with no additional clinical hours required in many cases.

- **Chamberlain University** — flexible online RN-to-BSN.
- **Western Governors University** — competency-based RN-to-BSN, self-paced.
- **University of Illinois Chicago (UIC)** — online RN-to-BSN from a public research university.
- **Texas A&M University** — online RN-to-BSN.
- **Eastern Florida State College** — online RN-to-BSN.
- **Aspen University** — online RN-to-BSN.
- **Southern University and A&M College** — online RN-to-BSN.
- **West Coast University** — online RN-to-BSN pathway.

**What to check before applying to any online nursing program:**

- Is the program accredited by CCNE (Commission on Collegiate Nursing Education) or ACEN (Accreditation Commission for Education in Nursing)? Accreditation is non-negotiable.
- Does the program operate in your state? Many online nursing programs have state-specific enrollment restrictions.
- How does the program handle clinical placements? Do they arrange them, or are you responsible for finding a site?
- What are the NCLEX first-time pass rates for graduates?
- Are the prerequisites the same as traditional programs? Most online programs require the same science prerequisites — they don't waive them.

<div class="section-label">Degree Comparison</div>

## ADN vs. BSN vs. ABSN prerequisites compared

The type of nursing degree you're pursuing significantly shapes your prerequisite path.

### ADN — Associate Degree in Nursing

Typically offered at community colleges. Focused on essential science and general education requirements. Faster path to licensure (2 years post-prerequisites) at lower upfront cost. Some employers prefer or require BSN for advancement.

### BSN — Bachelor of Science in Nursing

Requires more general education credits and has more competitive admissions. Often requires 60+ prerequisite credits. Broader career advancement options and required by many hospital systems and Magnet facilities.

### ABSN — Accelerated BSN

Designed for students who already have a bachelor's degree in another field. Fast-paced (typically 12–18 months). Science GPA is scrutinized heavily. All prerequisites are usually required before applying. Ideal for career changers.

<div class="section-label">Common Mistakes</div>

## 7 mistakes that delay nursing school applications by a year

1. **Taking courses before checking requirements.** Not all nursing programs accept the same courses or providers. Always verify first.
2. **Assuming every online class transfers.** Online science courses — especially without labs — are often rejected by traditional nursing programs.
3. **Ignoring lab requirements.** A lecture-only biology course will not satisfy a "Biology with lab" prerequisite, even if it covers the same content.
4. **Ignoring expiration dates.** A&P from 8 years ago may not count. Verify recency requirements before assuming old coursework will transfer.
5. **Taking classes out of sequence.** Biology before Chemistry, A&P I before A&P II — sequence matters both academically and for admissions point systems.
6. **Underestimating minimum grade requirements.** Many programs require a B or better in science prerequisites. A passing C may not qualify.
7. **Not rechecking requirements before applying.** Programs update their requirements. A course that qualified two years ago may no longer meet current standards.

<div class="section-label">Planning Guide</div>

## Step-by-step planning guide

1. **Make a list of your target nursing programs.** Be specific — include school name, degree type, and location. Aim for 3–6 programs.
2. **Pull each program's official prerequisite page.** Go to the nursing school's admissions page directly. Note every course, its lab requirement, minimum grade, and any expiration policy.
3. **Identify the overlap and the gaps.** Build a spreadsheet. Which courses appear on every school's list? Start with those.
4. **Meet with an academic advisor or college counselor.** A counselor can confirm transfer policies, help you build a semester-by-semester schedule, and flag courses that won't transfer.
5. **Start science prerequisites as early as possible.** Biology, Chemistry, and A&P are sequenced — the sooner you start, the more flexibility you have.
6. **Maintain strong grades in every science course.** A strong science GPA gives you options; a weak one narrows them significantly.
7. **Recheck requirements in the semester before you apply.** Program requirements change. Confirm everything is current before submitting applications.
