## Radiologic Technology at El Camino College

If you are researching **radiologic technology** (sometimes shortened to “radiology tech” in casual conversation), start with ECC’s official **Radiologic Technology** division pages. The college’s **[Radiologic Technology program](https://www.elcamino.edu/academics/divisions/health-care/radiologic-technology/index.php)** describes a structured pathway that includes coursework, labs, and clinical education meant to prepare students for professional standards in medical imaging—including eligibility expectations connected to credentialing pathways such as the ARRT registry exam (confirm current rules with ECC and ARRT).

ECC also publishes **[degree and certificate details](https://www.elcamino.edu/academics/divisions/health-care/radiologic-technology/degree-certificate.php)** for the program, including the **26‑month** course sequence overview and accreditation notes. The program is accredited by JRCERT (Joint Review Committee on Education in Radiologic Technology)—an important quality signal for prospective imaging students comparing programs nationwide.

**Important distinction:** ECC’s Radiologic Technology pathway is **not** marketed as a standalone, fully online bachelor’s degree in radiology. Community college radiologic technology programs typically blend **face‑to‑face labs**, **clinical placements**, and **some online or hybrid coursework** depending on the term and section—always confirm the latest modality on ECC’s official pages before you budget time or relocate.

## What is El Camino College?

El Camino College is a **public California community college** serving Torrance and surrounding South Bay cities. ECC reports a broad mission focused on access, equity, student success, transfer preparation, and workforce education. According to ECC’s general overview materials, the college offers **about 200 degree and certificate programs** and enrolls **roughly 33,000 students each semester**, making it one of the region’s busiest community college campuses.{' '}
<a class="link-external" href="https://www.elcamino.edu/about/index.php" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">About El Camino College</a>

Physically, ECC’s campus is a South Bay landmark: ECC describes about **126 acres** of instructional space and student services—large enough to support athletics, labs, and extensive career programs alongside transfer pathways.{' '}
<a class="link-external" href="https://www.elcamino.edu/about/index.php" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">ECC Fast Facts / About</a>

## A brief history of ECC

El Camino College traces its origins to **1947**, when growing postwar enrollment pressure in the South Bay helped justify a new community college district. ECC notes that the district was officially established **July 1, 1947**, with early classes held in temporary wartime structures before permanent facilities took shape—a story that still shapes ECC’s identity as a “community-built” institution tied to regional growth.{' '}
<a class="link-external" href="https://www.elcamino.edu/about/history.php" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">ECC History</a>

The name **El Camino** references **El Camino Real**, the historic mission-era route remembered throughout California; ECC’s bell imagery connects to that heritage. Over decades, modernization and bond-supported improvements helped ECC expand facilities while preserving its core role as an affordable transfer and workforce engine for the region.{' '}
<a class="link-external" href="https://www.elcamino.edu/leadership/bond/faq.php" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">ECC Bond program FAQ</a>

## ECC today

Modern ECC blends **transfer pathways** (CSU/UC-oriented preparation where applicable), **career education**, and **distance education** options designed for students balancing jobs and families. ECC maintains a dedicated **online education** hub explaining how remote coursework works alongside on-campus expectations—useful context if you want flexibility but still need occasional in-person requirements for labs or proctored assessments.{' '}
<a class="link-external" href="https://www.elcamino.edu/academics/online-education/index.php" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">ECC Online Education</a>

Because ECC serves multiple cities across its district footprint, it functions less like a single-neighborhood school and more like a **regional educational anchor**—especially for students who want affordable units before transferring to a four-year university.

## Is ECC’s imaging pathway fully online?

**No—don’t plan on a 100% online bachelor’s in radiology at ECC.** El Camino College’s **Radiologic Technology** pathway is designed around professional standards that require **hands-on labs** and **clinical training** at affiliated sites. ECC’s program materials describe a **26‑month** progression that includes classroom learning, laboratory practice, and clinical assignments—exact meeting patterns can vary by cohort.{' '}
<a class="link-external" href="https://www.elcamino.edu/academics/divisions/health-care/radiologic-technology/degree-certificate.php" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">ECC Radiologic Technology — degree & certificate</a>

What you *can* reasonably expect is a **hybrid experience**: some theory or support coursework may be offered online or with flexible components (verify each semester’s schedule), while competency-based clinical education still requires verified hours in real healthcare environments.

If your goal is **ARRT-aligned training** through a California community college, ECC’s **[Radiologic Technology](https://www.elcamino.edu/academics/divisions/health-care/radiologic-technology/index.php)** pages are the authoritative starting point. If your goal is a **fully online bachelor’s** with zero clinical components, you will likely need a different institutional model—those programs are uncommon in imaging for good reason (patient safety and licensure standards).
